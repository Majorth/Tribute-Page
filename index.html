Bryan Ferry

"pop's original art-school bobby-dazzler" GQ

  • 1945: Born in Washington, County Durham, England
  • 1964-68: He attended Newcastle University where he studied art and was member of several bands.
  • 1970: Roxy Music is formed.
  • 1972: Roxy Music's first hit, "Virgina Plan", made the UK top 5.
  • 1973-74: These Foolish Things and Another Time, Another Place reach the UK top 5.
  • 1976-1978: Roxy Music disbanded and Bryan Ferry went on tour, releasing three solo albumns all of which charted in the UK top 20.
  • 1980 & 1982: Flesh + Blood and Avalon hit #1 in the UK album charts.
  • 1983:Roxy Music disbands. Bryan Ferry embarks upon a solo career.
  • 1985: Boys and Girls reaches #1 on UK album charts and remained on the chart for 44 weeks.
  • 1987: Bete Noir is released with a subsequent tour the following year.
  • 1993: Ferry released Taxi and commenced its tour.
  • 2001: Ferry re-reformed Roxy Music with his colleagues and went on tour.
  • 2004:
    • Ferry starred in a short film The Porter.
  • 2007: Ferry released a tribute to Bob Dylan, Dylanesque.
  • 2010: Ferry released Olympia, which featured model Kate Moss on the album cover.
  • 2011: Roxy Music peformed one last time, and Ferry was made CBE in the Queen's Birthday Honours.
  • 2012: The Jazz Age is released.
  • 2014: Avonore is released.
  • 2015: Ferry released the 45th anniversary deluxe edition of Roxy Music's eponymous debut albumn.
  • 2019: Ferry and his orchestra went on a world tour in 2019.
Personal interactions with Ferry are a "bespoke event, louche ensemble of elegant affectations." Esquire
